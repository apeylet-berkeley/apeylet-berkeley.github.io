Part 1 — Selfie: wrong vs right method

First too close with wide angle, then backed up + zoomed to keep the face size but with natural proportions. A third image can show another variation.

Why different? Backing up changes projection geometry: rays are more parallel and depth relations (nose/ears) are less exaggerated. Zoom adjusts field of view without re-introducing distortion.

Part 2 — Architectural perspective compression

Take the same street/axis: first zoomed from far (flattened), then wide angle up close (strong convergence), while keeping apparent subject scale similar.

Interpretation: Distance modifies depth ratios. Telephoto “compresses” planes, wide angle “expands” them.

Part 3 — Dolly Zoom (GIF/Video)

Sequence of 4–8 shots while backing up and zooming in to keep subject size constant. Combined into a short GIF/video.

Tip: export a GIF (< 8 MB) for gallery and an MP4 (H.264) for smooth playback.

Technical Notes & GIF Creation

Gear & Settings (example)

  • Camera: smartphone or mirrorless; tripod recommended.
  • Mode: photo; lock exposure for dolly zoom sequence.
  • Focal: transition from wide to telephoto while backing up.
  • Framing: keep subject same size across shots.

Create a GIF (ImageMagick/FFmpeg)

magick convert -delay 8 -loop 0 media/part3/frames/*.jpg -resize 1280x720 -layers Optimize media/part3/dollyzoom.gif

ffmpeg -framerate 12 -pattern_type glob -i 'media/part3/frames/*.jpg'   -vf scale=1280:-2 -c:v libx264 -pix_fmt yuv420p -crf 20 media/part3/dollyzoom.mp4
